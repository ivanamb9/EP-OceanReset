About West Vancouver

West Vancouver is a quiet coastal district on the North Shore known for its ocean views, forested parks, and calm atmosphere. Unlike busy downtown Vancouver, West Van offers peaceful natural spaces that feel far from city noise. With spots like Lighthouse Park, Whytecliff Park, and the Horseshoe Bay lookout, the area is known for providing easy, accessible escapes where people can relax, enjoy nature, and recharge.

How being in nature helps reduce stress

Spending time in nature has been shown to lower stress by calming the body and mind. Natural environments reduce sensory overload, slow down heart rate, and help the brain switch out of “busy” thinking. The sounds of waves, wind, and birds can trigger relaxation, while greenery and open views reduce mental fatigue. Even short visits to quiet outdoor spaces can improve mood, increase focus, and make people feel more refreshed and balanced.

Why Breathing Matters

Breathing is one of the simplest and fastest ways to calm the mind. Slow, deep breaths tell the body to relax, lowering stress levels, easing tension, and improving focus. When you breathe deeply, your nervous system shifts out of “stress mode,” helping you feel more grounded and present.

Try this simple breathing exercise designed to help slow down, reset, and enjoy the calm atmosphere of each hidden oceanfront spot. It only takes a few seconds and can be done anywhere.

01

Find a peaceful place where you won’t be disturbed for about 2–5 minutes and get into a comfortable position.

02

Take a moment to get comfortable and focus on your emotions. Rest a hand on your chest or stomach if it helps you feel calm.

03

Inhale through your nose, counting four heartbeats. 1, 2, 3, 4.

04

Hold your breath, counting two heartbeats. 1, 2.

05

Exhale through your nose, counting six heartbeats. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6.

06

Repeat this for 2–5 minutes, or if you are using this exercise to help manage a trigger, repeat these steps as many times as it takes for you to feel grounded in the present moment. As your heartbeats slow, your breathing will get deeper, continuing to calm you.
