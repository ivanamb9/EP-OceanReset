Whytecliff Park

What is Whytecliff Park?

Whytecliff Park is one of West Vancouver’s most scenic waterfront areas, known for its rocky shoreline, clear ocean views, and calm island trails. It’s a peaceful place to sit by the water, watch the waves, and enjoy quiet coastal scenery. The main highlight is Whyte Islet, a small island you can walk to during low tide.

Brief History

Whytecliff Park was established in the early 1900s and became one of West Vancouver’s first official parks in 1914. It later grew in popularity as a diving spot due to its marine life and protected waters. The iconic Whyte Islet became a digitally recognized protected natural feature in Whytecliff Park Marine Protected Area, helping preserve the wildlife and quiet character of the area.

Best Hours to Visit

  • Early evenings for quiet, calm ocean views
  • Weekdays for fewer people
  • Low tide if you want to walk to Whyte Islet

What to Expect

  • Beautiful ocean cliffs and rocky beaches
  • Wide views of Howe Sound and nearby islands
  • Gentle walking paths and places to sit
  • Tide pools and wildlife (seals are common)

Crowds

  • Weekends can get busy near the main beach
  • The cliffs and upper trails stay quieter
  • Sunrise/sunset hours are peaceful but popular

What to Bring

  • Good shoes (rocks can be slippery)
  • Water and snacks
  • A light jacket (the ocean side can be windy)
  • Phone/camera for photos
  • Optional: a blanket or journal if you plan to sit and relax

Location

Whytecliff Park is located near Horseshoe Bay in West Vancouver, about a 30-minute drive from downtown Vancouver. It sits at the edge of Howe Sound, giving it wide views, fresh ocean air, and a relaxing atmosphere away from city noise.
