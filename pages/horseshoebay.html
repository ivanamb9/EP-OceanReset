Horseshoe Bay Lookout

What is Horseshoe Bay Lookout?

Horseshoe Bay Lookout is a quiet, cliff-side viewpoint overlooking the ocean, islands, and the ferry terminal far below. It’s known for being peaceful, hidden, and perfect for anyone wanting a calm place to breathe, reset, and enjoy wide ocean views without crowds. It’s a short walk from the road and feels like a private escape above the water.

Brief History

The lookout formed naturally along the rocky cliffs above Horseshoe Bay and became a quiet local viewpoint as the village grew in the mid-1900s. It has never been an official park, more of a local hidden gem used by residents to watch the sunset, breathe ocean air, and enjoy a peaceful break from the village below.

Best Hours to Visit

  • Sunset → the entire bay glows orange
  • Weekday afternoons for total quiet
  • Early mornings for soft light and empty views

What to Expect

  • A calm, elevated ocean viewpoint
  • Quiet atmosphere with minimal foot traffic
  • Views of Bowen Island, ferries, and cliffs
  • A short, easy walk from the street

Crowds

  • Very low — it’s not a popular tourist spot
  • You might only see a few locals passing by
  • Evenings can attract photographers, but still quiet

What to Bring

  • Water
  • Water and snacks
  • A light jacket (the ocean side can be windy)
  • Phone/camera for photos
  • Good shoes for uneven ground

Location

Located just above Horseshoe Bay Village in West Vancouver, the lookout sits along a small residential hill with a clear view of Howe Sound. It’s 30–35 minutes from downtown Vancouver, close to Whytecliff Park and the ferry terminal, but tucked away from busy areas.
